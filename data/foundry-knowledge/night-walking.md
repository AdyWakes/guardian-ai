# Walking Alone at Night

Guidance for someone walking alone at night or in low-light conditions.

## Steps

1. Stick to well-lit, populated routes even if they are longer than the alternative.
2. Keep your phone accessible but avoid being visually distracted by the screen.
3. Walk confidently and with purpose. Body language matters — predators select targets who appear unaware or hesitant.
4. Avoid isolated areas and shortcuts through dark spaces such as alleys, empty parks, or wooded paths.
5. Keep one ear free if you use headphones. Lower the volume so you can hear footsteps and traffic.
6. Carry a personal alarm or whistle that can be triggered without unlocking your phone.
7. Have your keys ready in your hand before you reach your destination so you do not pause to dig in a bag at the door.
