# Domestic Situation Escalation

Guidance for someone in a domestic situation that is becoming unsafe — a partner, household member, or family conflict turning hostile or violent.

## Steps

1. De-escalate verbally if it is safe to do so. Use a calm, non-confrontational tone. Avoid challenging or correcting in the moment.
2. Identify safe exits and plan your escape route now. Mentally rehearse the path from where you are standing to the nearest exit.
3. Keep your phone charged and accessible. Memorize at least one emergency number in case you cannot unlock the phone.
4. Have a code word agreed with trusted contacts. Sending the word means "call 911 to my address" without further explanation.
5. If violence occurs, protect your head and vital organs. Curl up, cover with your arms, and put a barrier (furniture, door) between you and the attacker if possible.
6. Call 911 as soon as it is safe. Even a connected silent call can dispatch officers if your number is recognized.
7. Your safety is the priority. Property, belongings, and the relationship come second.
